Amitabh Bachchan

Amitabh Bachchan is an iconic Bollywood actor known as the "Shahenshah" of Indian cinema. Rising to fame in the 1970s with films like "Zanjeer" and "Sholay," he has appeared in over 200 films and won numerous awards, including National Film Awards.

Early Life

Birth:

Amitabh Bachchan was born on October 11, 1942, in Allahabad, India, into a family deeply rooted in literature and the arts. His father, Harivansh Rai Bachchan, was a renowned poet, and his mother, Teji Bachchan, was a social activist.

Education:

He completed his schooling in Allahabad and graduated from the University of Delhi, where he studied Science. He later pursued a degree in Arts.

Career Beginnings

Early Struggles:

Before entering the film industry, Bachchan worked as a voice narrator for All India Radio and later tried his luck in acting. His initial roles were not very successful.

Breakthrough:

His big break came in 1973 with the film "Zanjeer," where he portrayed a tough police officer, earning him the title of the "angry young man" of Bollywood.

Film Career

Iconic Films:

Amitabh has starred in numerous blockbuster films, including "Sholay," "Deewar," "Paa," "Kabhi Khushi Kabhie Gham," and "Chupke Chupke." He is known for his versatility, portraying a wide range of characters.

Awards and Honors:

Throughout his career, he has won numerous awards, including the National Film Awards, Filmfare Awards, and the Dadasaheb Phalke Award for lifetime achievement.

Sachin Tendulkar

Sachin Ramesh Tendulkar is celebrated as one of the greatest cricketers in history. He made his international debut at the age of 16 in 1989 and went on to become the highest run-scorer in both Test and One Day International (ODI) cricket, with over 34,000 runs and 100 international centuries.

Early Life

Birth:

Sachin Ramesh Tendulkar was born on April 24, 1973, in Mumbai, India.

Family Background:

Growing up in a middle-class family, his father, Ramesh Tendulkar, was a notable Marathi novelist, while his mother, Rajni, worked in the insurance sector.

Cricketing Beginnings:

Sachin displayed remarkable talent for cricket at a young age, starting his journey at 11. Under the mentorship of former cricketer Ramakant Achrekar, he made his debut for the Mumbai school team.

International Career

Debut:

Sachin made his international debut at the age of 16, playing his first Test match against Pakistan on November 15, 1989. He became the youngest player to score a Test century.

Career Highlights:

Over an illustrious 24-year career, Tendulkar became the highest run-scorer in the history of both Test and One Day International (ODI) cricket.

A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was a renowned Indian aerospace scientist, educator, and politician who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He is often referred to as the "Missile Man of India" for his pivotal role in India's missile development programs and is celebrated as one of the most inspiring figures in Indian history.

Early Life

Birth:

Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, Abdul Kalam came from a humble background. His father was a boat owner, and his mother was a homemaker.

Education:

Kalam excelled academically, earning a degree in physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, and later pursuing aerospace engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology.

Career in Science and Technology

ISRO and DRDO:

Leadership Roles:

He held various significant positions, including serving as the Chief Scientific Advisor to the Prime Minister and as Secretary of the Defence Research and Development Organisation.

Presidency

11th President of India:

Dr. Kalam served as President from 2002 to 2007, earning the nickname "People's President" for his approachable nature and commitment to youth and education.

Vision for India:

During his presidency, he advocated for the vision of India as a developed nation by 2020, emphasizing the importance of technology and education.
